Maintenance Tips

Cleaning: Regularly clean your phone with a soft, lint-free cloth to remove dust and debris. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive materials that could damage the screen or casing.

Protective Accessories: Use a sturdy case/flip case and screen protector to shield your phone from drops, scratches, and other physical damage.

Temperature and Moisture: Avoid exposing your phone to extreme temperatures (hot or cold) and moisture, as these can negatively impact battery life and internal components.

Handling: Be mindful of how you handle your phone. Avoid dropping it, placing it in hot environments, or exposing it to liquids.

Charging Practices: Avoid letting your battery drain completely or charging it to 100% all the time. Keeping the charge between 20% and 80% is generally recommended for optimal battery health, according to Textmagic.

Charger: Use the original charger provided with your device or a certified alternative. Using incompatible chargers can damage the battery.

Overcharging: Unplug your phone once it reaches 100% to avoid overcharging, even though modern phones have charging protections.

Battery-Draining Apps: Limit the use of battery-draining apps and games, especially when your battery is low.

Power Saving Mode: Utilize battery-saving modes or low power mode when your battery is running low.

Software Updates: Keep your phone's operating system updated. Updates often include security patches, performance improvements, and bug fixes.

App Management: Regularly uninstall apps you no longer use and clear the cache to free up storage space and improve performance.

Data Backups: Back up your important data regularly to protect against data loss due to hardware failure or other issues.

Storage Space: Monitor your phone's storage space. Running out of space can slow down your phone.

Don't Overload: Avoid keeping too many apps open in the background. Close apps you are not actively using to save resources.

Limit Notifications: Disable push notifications for non-essential apps to reduce distractions and save battery.

Use Headphones or Speakers: Reduce strain on your phone's speaker by using headphones or a speakerphone when possible.

Seek Professional Help: If you encounter any issues or your phone requires repairs, seek professional help from a reputable repair service here.
